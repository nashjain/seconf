SeConf 2014

4th Annual Selenium Conference

Sep 4-6th 2014, Bangalore

The Selenium Conference is a volunteer-run, non-profit event put on by members of the Selenium Community that brings together Selenium developers & enthusiasts from around the world to share ideas, socialize, and work together on advancing the present and future success of the project.

The conference begins Thursday with all-day workshops, followed by two days of presentations by experts and practitioners from around the world. The conference will host 2 parallel tracks. There will also be pair-coding sessions throughout the day, as well as opportunities to get Selenium advice and tutorials from experts.

Call for Proposals

Interested in presenting at SeConf 14? The public proposal submission system will go live on May 15th.

REVIEW PROCESS

Interested speakers are requested to submit their proposals directly on our proposal submission system. All proposals will be public. Registered user of the submission system will be able to comment on your proposal. Submitters may also post comments on reviews or public comments of their own proposals to provide clarifications, explain revisions and respond to questions. Comments by public users are information that can be utilized by both the submitter and the review team. Ultimately the decision to accept a session resides with the program team, the program chair, and the conference chair.

Your proposal stands the best chance to be picked up, if it's fully flushed, ready-to-go. Ensure you provide links to:

  • previous conference talks
  • open source project contributions
  • slides & videos of your presentations
  • your blog posts or articles
  • and so on...
